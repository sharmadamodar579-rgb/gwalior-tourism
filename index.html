The Royal Tapestry of Gwalior

Gwalior's history is woven with the deeds of great dynasties—from the Tomars and Mughals to the Marathas and Scindias. Each has left an indelible mark on the landscape, food, music, and architecture of this majestic region.

Did You Know?

  • The Zero Connection: Gopachal Parvat and Gwalior Fort walls house some of the oldest stone inscriptions depicting the symbol "0" (zero) in temples.
  • World's Largest Chandeliers: The Durbar Hall in Jai Vilas Palace holds two chandeliers weighing 3.5 tons each. Ten elephants were climbed onto the roof to test its load capacity before installation.
  • The Sweet Voice Tamarind: Legend has it that chewing the leaves of the tamarind tree near Tansen's tomb cures throat ailments and sweetens a singer's voice.
